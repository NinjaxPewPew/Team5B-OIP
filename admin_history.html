History

Community-Stained Glass Window

View All Images 

Play Audio 

In 2008, a community-stained glass window was created by the local artists group Red Dot Art, funded by Glasgow City Council. The window aimed to inspire the people of Partick to reintroduce stained-glass panels into the area. The design, depicting the four seasons and symbolizing the Annexe's year-round support for the community, was well-received. Though not fully achieving the vision, some stained glass pieces now grace windows in Partick and Whiteinch. The artist behind the window, David Stevenson, and other contributors (Graeme, Brendan and Liz) are remembered for their legacies even after their passing.
